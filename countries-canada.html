PermanentResidency in Canada

A permanent resident is not a Canadian citizen but someone who has been given permanent resident status by immigrating to Canada. Permanent residents are citizens of other countries. A person in Canada temporarily, like a student or foreign worker, is not a permanent resident.


As a permanent resident, individuals have certain rights:


  1. They are entitled to most of social benefits that Canadians receive.
  2. They have health care coverage through the public health system.
  3. They can live, work or study wherever they desire across Canada.
  4. They can apply for Canadian Citizenship, based on the regulations.
  5. They are protected by the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms.
  6. They must abide by all of the federal, provincial and municipal laws.

Permanent Residents are allowed to reside outside Canada, but must reside in Canada at least 2 years during a 5-year period. Permanent Residency Status may be lost, if they decide to reside outside Canada for long time.

EXPRESS ENTRY IMMIGRATION

Express Entry is an immigration stream for permanent resident applicants. Express Entry falls under the following three federal immigration programs:


Federal Skilled Workers


Federal Skilled Traders


Canadian Experience Class






It is possible to apply for provinces and territories under this stream where immigrant candidates can become eligible for PR through Provincial Nominee Programs – these are designed to fulfill labour needs of all the regions.

PROVINCIAL NOMINEE PROGRAM



The Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs) allow Canadian provinces and territories to nominate individuals who wish to immigrate to Canada and who are interested in settling in a particular province


Each Canadian province and territory (except Nunavut and Quebec) has its own unique Provincial Nominee Program. Each PNP has at least one immigration stream that is aligned with the federal Express Entry immigration selection system.


Provincial nominations issued under these Express Entry-aligned streams are known as ‘enhanced nominations’ and award Express Entry candidates an additional 600 Comprehensive Ranking System(CRS) points.

ATLANTIC IMMIGRATION PILOT PROGRAMME

The Atlantic Immigration Pilot is a pathway to permanent residence for skilled foreign workers and international graduates who want to work and live in one of Canada’s 4 Atlantic Provinces:


  1. This is an employer-driven program designed to help employers in Atlantic Canada hire qualified candidates for jobs they have been unable to fill locally.

  2. To immigrate to Atlantic Canada through the pilot, candidate must be a recent graduate of a publically funded institution in Atlantic Canada or a skilled worker who meets the program requirements.

  3. The candidate can be living abroad or already be in Canada temporarily.

  4. The candidate must receive a job offer from a designated employer in Atlantic Canada to participate in the pilot.
